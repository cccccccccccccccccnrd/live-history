Flow study

The flow study makes use of wider more birds-eye-view shots of bigger crossroads to analyze traffic flow behaviour. Here it is interesting to see the groupings and intersections of the different devices. Within the different locations there can be seen similar patterns and movement sequences when looking at the flow of traffic. Not only in the form of video material, but even in an abstract presentation, it is satisfying to see how complex our traffic system is and that most of the time we perceive only a little piece of it.

Perspective study

The perspective study uses footage filmed while moving forward using different vehicles to showcase traffic experiences from the perspective of various participants. Revealing the correlations of speed and distinctions of positioning. The distribution of the physical public space never is a fixed circumstance, it is a constantly changing process whose perception is always individual.

Device study

The device study is telling the story of hierarchy and organisation of the distribution of space in traffic through dissecting the individual groups of devices. The study is based on the same footage as the flow study, but showing four visualizations of the same perspective. Through the deconstruction of traffic, individual device patterns and behaviours become apparent.
